Scientific talk · Educational Data Science

Making Learning Processes Visible

Educational Data Science for Writing, AI Feedback, and Large-Scale Assessment

Thorben Jansen TU Dortmund · IFS June 9, 2026

Problem

Students use AI, but data is rarely used to improve quality of education and learning.

AI is already in the process

Students prompt, copy, paste, evaluate, accept, reject, and integrate AI suggestions while writing.

Assessment sees only the products

Most assessment still focuses on final products, not on how students used AI or revised their texts.

Teachers need usable evidence

Teachers need interpretable process information to decide what feedback or instruction should come next.

Connection between theory and data

Process traces only become educational evidence when they are linked to learning theory and subject criteria.

Contribution to the problem

I build the bridge from educational questions to data-scientific evidence.

Foreign-language argumentation

2,314 TOEFL argumentative essays from year 11 students.

  • Students from Germany and Switzerland.
  • Two measurement points with analytic and holistic expert ratings.
  • Criteria: content, organization, language quality, and holistic score.
Keller, Lohmann, Trüb, Fleckenstein, Meyer, Jansen, & Möller, 2024

PISA data source

PISA gives a controlled window into AI-supported writing.

  • 1,699 students from Germany, age 15.
  • Two texts, 30 minutes each: one without AI support and one with AI support.
  • Process data makes AI-supported writing inspectable.

PISA process evidence

Texts written with chatbots receive higher scores.

Higher distributions Chatbot-supported texts shift toward higher score ranges.
